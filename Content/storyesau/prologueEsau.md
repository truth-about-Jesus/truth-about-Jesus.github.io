*Why* should we write a story about 

我們為什麼要寫一個關於

\

Esau?

以掃？

\

Wasn't *Jacob*

不是雅各嗎

\

the son God chose 

上帝選擇的人

\

to become the progenitor of Israel,

成為以色列的祖先，

\

His chosen people?

祂的選民嗎？

\

Esau was just...

以掃只是......

\

his older brother...

他的哥哥......

\

wasn't he?

他不是嗎？

\

What can we *possibly* learn 

我們能學到什麼

\

from Esau's story?

來自以掃的故事？

\

<center>💠</center>

\
\

Perhaps we may view it as a cautionary tale,

也許我們可以把它看成是一個警示故事，

\

a negative example

一個負面的例子

\

of what *not* to do,

不該做什麼，

\

if one desires God's favour.

如果一個人想要上帝的恩惠。

\

Let's get started...

我們開始吧......

\

and find out.

並找出答案。

##### _Malachi 1:2-4 (GNB)_

###### \

2 The LORD says to his people, "I have always loved you." But they reply, "How have you shown your love for us?" 

The LORD answers, "Esau and Jacob were brothers, but I have loved Jacob and his descendants,
3 and have hated Esau and his descendants. I have devastated Esau's hill country and abandoned the land to jackals."

4 If Esau's descendants, the Edomites, say, "Our towns have been destroyed, but we will rebuild them," then the LORD will reply, "Let them rebuild—I will tear them down again. People will call them 'The evil country' and 'The nation with whom the LORD is angry forever.' "
